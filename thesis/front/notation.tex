\chapter*{Notation and Conventions}

Natural units \(\hbar = c = k_B = 1\) are used throughout. Newton's
gravitational constant \(G\) is kept explicit wherever it appears.
Energies and masses are expressed in \(\mathrm{MeV}\) or
\(\mathrm{GeV}\). Stellar masses are quoted in solar masses \(M_\odot\)
and radii in kilometres.

The metric signature is
\[
    (+,-,-,-),
    \qquad
    \eta_{\mu\nu} = \mathrm{diag}(1,-1,-1,-1).
\]
Repeated indices are summed (Einstein convention). Greek indices
\(\mu,\nu,\ldots\) run over \(0,1,2,3\); Latin indices \(i,j,\ldots\)
run over \(1,2,3\).

Dirac gamma matrices satisfy
\[
    \{\gamma^\mu,\gamma^\nu\} = 2\eta^{\mu\nu},
    \qquad
    \gamma^5 = i\gamma^0\gamma^1\gamma^2\gamma^3.
\]
Pauli matrices acting in flavor space are denoted \(\tau_i\) (or
\(\vec{\tau}\) for the isovector triplet). Color generators of
\(SU(3)\) in the fundamental representation are denoted \(T^A\).
Flavor, color, and spinor indices are suppressed when unambiguous.
